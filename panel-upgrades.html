What Nobody Tells You

Here's What Happens When an Outdated Panel Is Left Alone

Your electrical panel is the most important safety system in your home. It controls every circuit, protects every wire, and either safely manages your load — or it doesn't. Most homeowners have no idea there's a problem until it forces the conversation. And by then, the stakes are high.

🔥

Fire Risk You Can't See

Federal Pacific, Zinsco, and aging panels have documented breaker failure rates. A breaker that doesn't trip when it should is the leading cause of residential electrical fires. By the time you smell something, it's already too late.

📋

Insurance & Resale Consequences

Insurers actively flag or cancel policies on homes with outdated panels. If your home goes to market, the buyer's inspector will find it. A flagged panel can kill your deal — or cost tens of thousands in concessions. It shows up. Always.

⚡

Modern Homes Need More Power

EVs, solar, battery storage, smart appliances, modern HVAC. A 100-amp panel from 1990 wasn't engineered for today's electrical demand. Running it at capacity accelerates failure risk and limits everything you can do with your property.

Don't Ignore These

8 Signs Your Panel Needs an Upgrade Now

  • Your panel is 20+ years old
  • Breakers trip frequently or won't reset properly
  • Lights flicker or dim when appliances kick on
  • You have a Federal Pacific, Zinsco, or fuse-box panel
  • Your insurance carrier has flagged your electrical system
  • You're adding solar, an EV charger, or battery storage
  • You're planning to sell in the next 1–3 years
  • You rely on extension cords as permanent solutions

If any of these describe your home, this isn't a wait and see situation. A free assessment takes 30 minutes and gives you a clear, honest answer.
